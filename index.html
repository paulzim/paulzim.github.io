Paul Zimmerman

Content | Community | Creativity

Hi, I’m Paul Zimmerman. I’ve worn many hats throughout my career, from technical writer to program manager to senior leader, each step building my expertise in content strategy, community engagement, developer advocacy, and program management.

I’m passionate about empowering developers and creating thriving communities around cutting-edge technologies. Whether it’s leading international teams, delivering impactful workshops, or implementing scalable systems, I focus on innovation, empathy, and collaboration to achieve results.

My Journey

Content is the Foundation

My career began as a technical writer, developing clear and engaging documentation for emerging technologies. At companies like Octel Communications and Lucent Technologies, I honed my ability to translate complex ideas into accessible content and began managing teams to scale this work.

Pioneering New Platforms at Cisco

At Cisco, I took on program management roles where I launched groundbreaking projects like Cisco’s first collaborative documentation platform, the DocWiki. These efforts taught me the power of collaboration and community-building, laying the foundation for my later work in developer relations.

Elevating Developer Success

In 2015, I became Senior Manager of Developer Success, leading global teams to provide support for developers using Cisco’s platforms. I implemented new CRM systems, supported the launch of technical certifications, and earned the Pioneer Award for the DevNet Sandbox project.

Building Vibrant Communities

As Senior Manager of Developer Community Engagement, I focused on creating spaces where developers could thrive. From revamping forums and launching new technical hubs to piloting in-person meetups, I fostered connections that continue to drive innovation.

Leading with Vision in Developer Advocacy

Most recently, I led a team of developer advocates to bridge Cisco’s technologies with the broader developer ecosystem. Highlights include organizing Developer Sustainability Week, presenting at industry conferences, and building partnerships with local communities.

Why I Do What I Do

Throughout my career, I’ve been driven by a desire to make technology accessible and engaging for everyone. I believe that great content, thoughtful support, and vibrant communities are the keys to helping developers succeed.
