Content Creation and Management

Content Creation

Content has been the foundation of my career. My first role was a technical writer for Comtech Services, a technical documentation consulting firm. From there, I joined a startup, Touch Communications, where I continued to build my technical writing skills and learned the ups and downs of startup culture.

I moved on to Octel Communications, continuing as a writer and then tech docs manager, where I managed a large team of writers, editors, and production specialists. Here, I gained experience in online and embedded content.

With my move to Cisco, I returned to writing for a few years before transitioning into program management.

Program Management

As a program manager, I tracked departmental metrics using the Balanced Scorecard approach to align goals and measure impact.

My largest accomplishment as a PM was the Cisco DocWiki, a publicly-facing wiki for Cisco technical content. I collaborated with the team to establish the system and addressed issues like moderation, content quality, and compliance. I authored articles on this initiative and presented at conferences.

People Management

I became a documentation manager again and continued my focus on content. In the realm of developer content, I assisted the OpenDaylight open source project with the initial version of their wiki-based documentation. I also worked extensively with embedded content for SDKs.

As the Developer Content Manager for Cisco’s DevNet team, I delved deeply into developer content, where I got into the Open API spec and Git-based publishing toolchains. My focus on content continued through other roles, For example, as Senior Manager of Developer Success, I built a support knowledge base on our new CRMS. I contined to personally develop content as well, building various forms of video content, and published online content like blogs and community engagement content.

As a manager, I led the implementation of content management systems, including a Trisoft DITA-based XML CMS and a Git-based toolchain for publishing developer docs to the DevNet site.
